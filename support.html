22 A DAY

"22 A Day" refers to the alarming statistic that approximately 22 military veterans in the United States take their own lives each day. This figure highlights a critical mental health crisis among veterans, emphasizing the urgent need for increased support, resources, and intervention strategies to address PTSD, depression, and other challenges faced by those who have served in the military. The campaign often seeks to raise awareness, promote dialogue, and drive efforts to reduce veteran suicide rates.

Resources

If you or know someone who needs help, ask the tough question or reach out. There are many resources available to all Military personnel, Active, Reserve, or any veteran. Military Onesource is a great start for anyone seeking help.
